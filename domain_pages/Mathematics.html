Description Artificial Neural Networks (ANNs) have been successfully employed in approximating nonlinear functions and solving differential equations (DEs). Unlike traditional methods, ANNs do not assume any predefined functional relationship between input data and output, making them a powerful tool for function approximation. Recently, there has been a significant increase in research activities focused on using ANNs to enhance computational efficiency in option valuation by learning from the results of established option pricing models, such as the Black-Scholes and Heston models. The aim of this project is to train a student in the application of ANN tools for option pricing models.
